From Milan Malpensa to Verona

You'll land at Malpensa (MXP), which sits outside Milan proper — so getting to Verona is really a two-step journey, plus a private-transfer shortcut if you'd rather skip the train altogether.

Option A — Airport train + high-speed rail

MXP
Airport
Malpensa Express · ~50 min
MIL
Milano Centrale
Frecciarossa / Italo · 1h10–1h20
VRN
Verona P. Nuova

  1. Take the Malpensa Express from the airport

    Direct train from the airport terminal to Milano Centrale, running roughly every 30 minutes. Buy at the station kiosk or in advance via the Trenord app.

  2. Book Milano Centrale → Verona Porta Nuova

    Around 30 direct high-speed trains run daily, operated by Trenitalia (Frecciarossa) or Italo, with the fastest at roughly 1 hour 10 minutes. Book directly at trenitalia.com or italotreno.com — both have English booking and apps. Reserve ahead for August travel, since it's peak season and trains do sell out on popular departures.

  3. Arrive at Verona Porta Nuova

    The station sits about a 20-minute walk (or short taxi) from the historic center. Villa Arvedi is outside the city center, so plan for a taxi, rideshare, or arranged transfer from the station.

Option B — Private transfer, door to door

Skip Milan entirely: a private car from Malpensa straight to Verona takes about 2.5 hours by road. Worth it if you're landing late, traveling with a group, or just don't want to manage two train legs with luggage.
